Project X:

Context:

My personal project ‘Find ‘n Learn’

Project organisation:

Important for me was setting a clear goal and timeline. I was worried initially that the project would be too ambitious, but together with Dennis we set goals for the most important aspect of the project. Since this project isn’t focused on development, its end result will be a prototype instead of a fully coded interface. Testing and research is more important. Stemming from that, I set up a timeline in the research document that I followed thoroughly.

Explanatory research:

This project contained a lot of essential research. Important aspects were finding the issues students and teachers experienced in terms of information, asking questions and giving appropriate answers.

I made a document in which I set up the project. It first consists of literature study in which I find out what information is available to students and teachers. After that there are questions to be asked which roughly followed these lines in terms of research:

• Do people ask for questions a lot, what kind of questions are asked most often?

• Are the issues experienced with finding correct answers?

• But also, are there frustrations in having to ask teachers certain questions that might feel simple but require more effort?

This was the basis for the project and the basis for the first batch of interviews, according to the CMD methods it’s often best to interview around 5 people. It was important to make questions that would fit students and different questions of teachers, to get a different perspective.

I also conducted a survey from which I received some interesting answers. I printed out posters with QR codes to hang around the classroom and also spread the survey around on Teams. I expected that students would’ve had more questions about the ISSD or other student facilities. But it could be that students either rarely make use of them or it could be that they’re self explanatory.
